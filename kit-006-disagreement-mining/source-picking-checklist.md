# Source-Picking Checklist

The collision only works if each corpus actually states its author's system. Ten minutes here decides the quality of everything downstream.

## How many
- **2-3 thinkers.** Two gives you one axis of disagreement; three gives you triangulation (A vs. B fights often dissolve once C's framing is in the room). More than four muddies attribution.
- **3-4 pieces per thinker.** Fewer and the tool over-weights one essay's phrasing; more adds processing time without adding positions.
- **Keep the count balanced.** A 6-vs-2 load produces "contradictions" that are really just coverage gaps.

## What to load (in order of value)
1. **The canonical statement** — the author's own "what my system is" page or definitive overview (e.g., a "What is X" page, an introductory guide they still link to).
2. **The method mechanics** — one piece where they explain *how*, step by step. Contradictions live in procedure more than philosophy.
3. **A piece where they engage a rival** — if an author has written about another loaded author directly, load it. It's the richest contradiction ore (our run: Newport's essay on David Allen's universalism did more work than any other source).
4. **A recent piece** — positions drift; one current essay keeps the tool from arguing with a version of the author that no longer exists.

## What to avoid
- **Paywalled URLs** — they fail silently in most tools. Check your source count after adding; if it's short, find the author's own free companion piece (authors almost always blog their paywalled arguments).
- **Interviews and podcast transcripts** — conversational hedging dilutes stated positions. Essays state; interviews soften.
- **Third-party summaries** ("X's system explained") — you'd be mining a paraphraser's contradictions, not the author's.
- **Pieces with heavy comment sections** — fine to load, but when the tool quotes them, check the citation: a commenter's defense of the author is not the author. (Our run quoted commenters several times — correctly attributed, but easy to misread while skimming.)

## The balance test before you run
For each thinker, ask: "if a stranger read only these 3-4 pieces, could they state this person's system in two sentences?" If no for any thinker, swap sources until yes for all. Unequal fidelity produces fake wins for whoever was loaded best.
