# The Decompile Question Set

Run these against the full corpus, one at a time, in a long-context tool (NotebookLM or equivalent). Ask about *structure*, never content — you're extracting how the machine works, not what it says. Save every answer; they compile into `blueprint-template.md`.

Preamble to attach to every query:

> Answer only from patterns that recur across most of the corpus, not from single pieces. For every pattern, cite 2-3 concrete examples (title + the actual sentence/moment). If a pattern appears in fewer than ~70% of pieces, say "inconsistent" instead of forcing an answer.

## 1. The hook
How do the first three sentences of each piece work? What arrives first — a number, a story, a question, a claim? Where does the personal stake enter? Is there a repeatable shape? Quote five representative openings verbatim.

## 2. The structure
Map the skeleton of a typical piece: the ordered sections/beats from open to close. Is the skeleton fixed or variable? What signposting (headers, transitions, recurring phrases) marks the joints?

## 3. The ratio
Estimate the proportion of story/anecdote vs. framework/concept vs. direct instruction across the corpus. Does the ratio shift by topic? How are the modes interleaved — blocked or braided?

## 4. The moves
Within one major point, what's the recurring micro-pattern? (Example to test for: anchor story → named concept → concrete action.) How does the writer exit a point and enter the next?

## 5. The evidence style
How are claims supported — named studies, personal experience, historical events, analogies? How specific are the numbers? Are sources named inline, and with how much detail (researcher, year, institution)?

## 6. The voice
Sentence-length rhythm (measure a sample: short-punch frequency, longest-sentence habits). Register (warm/cold, blunt/hedged, self-deprecating/self-assured). Recurring vocabulary, signature phrases, sign-offs. What does this writer *never* do?

## 7. The close
How does every piece end? Is there a recurring final move (a zoom-out, a callback, a question, an ask)? Quote three closing paragraphs verbatim and describe the shared shape.

## Bonus passes (worth it on rich corpora)
- **Named frameworks:** list every coined framework/acronym/axiom, with its one-line definition. (These require attribution if you ever use them.)
- **The enemies list:** what does the writer consistently argue *against*? Rails-against material defines a voice as much as advocacy.
- **Titles:** what's the title formula? Length, structure, promise type, use of numbers.
