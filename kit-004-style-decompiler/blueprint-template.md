# [Writer Name] — Style & Craft Blueprint

> Distilled from [N] pieces ([date range]) via [tool] on [date]. One page max. If a section can't be filled from >70% of the corpus, mark it "inconsistent" — a blueprint padded with one-off observations produces generic drafts.

## 1. Persona & register
- Who the writer is on the page (credentials, vulnerabilities, the tension between them)
- Register in one line (e.g., "warm but blunt"; "dry, forensic, occasionally savage")
- Signature phrases / axioms / sign-off

## 2. The hook (first 3 sentences)
- The repeatable shape, stated as a formula
- 2-3 verbatim example openings
- What never appears in an opening

## 3. The skeleton
- The ordered beats of a typical piece, open to close
- Signposting habits (headers, transitions, recurring joints)

## 4. The ratio
- Story : framework : instruction, as percentages
- How the modes are interleaved

## 5. The moves (per major point)
- The micro-pattern within a point (e.g., anchor → concept → action)
- How points exit and hand off

## 6. Evidence style
- What counts as support (studies / experience / history / analogy) and in what mix
- Specificity habits (named researchers? years? exact numbers?)

## 7. The close
- The recurring final move, stated as a formula
- 1-2 verbatim closing examples

## Named frameworks (attribution required if used)
- [FRAMEWORK] — one-line definition
- ...

## Rails against
- The writer's consistent enemies (ideas, not people) — these define the voice's edges

## Quality checklist (derived from the above — every draft checks against this)
- [ ] Hook matches the formula in §2
- [ ] Ratio within ±10% of §4
- [ ] Every major point completes the §5 move
- [ ] Evidence meets the specificity bar in §6
- [ ] Close executes §7 without cringe
