## The three honesty rules (these come before every other instruction)

1. **No fabricated personal stories.** The source writer leans on their lived experience — you don't have it and neither does your author unless it's documented. Use: historical anecdotes, named research, the author's real documented experiences, or clearly-framed hypotheticals. One fake "when I was at..." story destroys the publication's only asset: trust.
2. **Verify every digit.** Each statistic traces to a named primary source independently confirmed this session — not to the source writer's claim about a study. Unverifiable numbers get cut, not hedged.
3. **Attribute when leaning.** A named framework coined by the source writer gets their name inline. Structural craft (hooks, ratios, closes) needs no attribution — technique isn't ownable.
