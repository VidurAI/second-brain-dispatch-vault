# Cold-Explain Drafting Runbook

The consumption→creation protocol. Five steps, in order, no skipping. The point of the sequence: generation happens *before* the sources (or a model) can reshape it — so what ships is provably yours, and what's borrowed is provably labeled.

## Step 0 — Mission sentence (10 seconds, before consuming)
Before any serious read/watch: **"I'm consuming this because I need to ___."**
Aims the session at output from the first minute. (Habit credited to the creator our second brain is built on — a 1.2M-subscriber ex-CEO.)

## Step 1 — Notes (during/after consuming)
Capture into your second brain as usual. Distill: TL;DR, the frameworks by name, the stats WITH their claimed sources (you'll verify later — never trust them yet), one key quote.

## Step 2 — The cold explain (15 minutes, the load-bearing step)
Close every source. Notes too. Write the idea from memory:
- 5 sentences minimum, out loud or on paper first if that's your medium
- No peeking — a peek converts retrieval into rereading, and rereading is the
  36/33% condition, not the 80% one (Karpicke & Roediger, 2008)
- Mark the gaps honestly: what you reached for and couldn't produce

**The sorting rule:** what survives the cold explain, you own — build the piece
from it. What didn't survive, you haven't internalized — either drop it or go
back to the source and treat it as a QUOTE/attribution (it's theirs, labeled),
never as your prose.

## Step 3 — Draft
Draft from the cold explain, not from the notes. The notes re-enter only as:
- verification targets (every digit → verify-digit-workflow.md)
- attribution checks (every borrow → attribution-decision-tree.md)
- quotes, labeled as quotes

If you use a model to draft: the cold explain is the prompt's core material,
and the model is banned from adding claims that aren't in it. AI amplifies and
polishes; it does not originate. (The MIT preprint's warning is exactly this:
83.3% of LLM-assisted writers couldn't quote their own fresh essay — outsource
generation and the labels fall off.)

## Step 4 — Adversarial critique
A second pass — a person, or a critic prompt (Kit 004 ships one) — scores the
draft and specifically hunts:
- unattributed frameworks/claims (tree violations)
- unverified digits (workflow violations)
- first-person that isn't yours (instant reject)
- your own honesty rules, whatever you wrote in the template

Gate: nothing ships below your bar. Cap revision at two cycles — perfectionism
kills cadence, and a published archive compounds while a perfect draft doesn't.

## The weekly loop, compressed
Mission sentence → consume → note → cold explain → draft from the explain →
verify digits → attribution pass → critic gate → ship. Overhead beyond normal
drafting: ~1 hour. That hour is the difference between publishing *from* your
sources and publishing *as* them.
