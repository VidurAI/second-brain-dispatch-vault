## The four rules (ours — adapt, don't inherit blindly)

### 1. The first cut is always mine.
Every piece starts with a closed-book explanation of the idea, from memory, before anything gets drafted — by me or by a model. What survives the cold explain is what I own. What doesn't, I haven't earned the right to publish yet.

### 2. Verify every digit.
No statistic ships unless I chased it to a primary source myself this session. A creator citing a study is not a source; find the study. If I can't verify it, it gets dropped — not hedged, dropped.

*Your version:* ___________________

### 3. Attribute ideas; synthesize structures.
Named frameworks, coined terms, and specific claims from a source get credited inline, every time. Craft — hook shapes, ratios, closing moves — is technique, and technique isn't ownable. When unsure, run the attribution decision tree.

### 4. Precommit, don't detect.
I do not trust myself to notice borrowing at publish time (nobody can — that's the subconscious-plagiarism lesson). These rules are enforced by the pipeline as hard gates, not by my judgment under deadline.
