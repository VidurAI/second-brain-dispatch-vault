# Attribution Decision Tree

Two-minute call at every borrow point. Work top to bottom; first match wins.

```
Is it a VERBATIM PASSAGE (sentences, a distinctive phrase, a melody)?
├── YES → Quote it, name the source, link it. Long passages: don't — summarize
│         instead (summaries must be shorter than and different from the source).
└── NO ↓

Is it a NAMED FRAMEWORK, coined term, or acronym someone created?
├── YES → Use it freely, credit the creator inline, every appearance.
│         (Legally you don't have to — Circular 33: ideas/methods aren't
│         copyrightable. That's exactly why this is YOUR rule, not the law's.)
└── NO ↓

Is it a SPECIFIC CLAIM or statistic you learned from a source?
├── YES → Two obligations, not one:
│         1. Verify it at the primary source (verify-digit-workflow.md).
│         2. Cite the PRIMARY source in your receipts — and if the framing/
│            selection came from an intermediary (a video, a newsletter),
│            credit the intermediary for the framing too.
└── NO ↓

Is it a STORY OR LIVED EXPERIENCE that isn't yours?
├── YES → Tell it in third person with the person named, or don't tell it.
│         NEVER migrate someone else's anecdote into your first person —
│         this is the one unforgivable move.
└── NO ↓

Is it TECHNIQUE — a hook shape, a ratio, a structural move, a closing pattern?
├── YES → Use it. No attribution needed. Technique isn't ownable; writers
│         have apprenticed on structure since Franklin rebuilt the Spectator.
└── NO ↓

Is it an IDEA you can no longer trace ("I just know this")?
└── This is the Harrison zone. Untraceable + deeply-internalized source =
    assume it's theirs. Spend 5 minutes searching your notes/corpus. Found
    it → route back through this tree. Can't find it → either generalize it
    beyond recognition-by-you ("several thinkers argue...") or drop it.
    When in doubt: attribute. Over-crediting costs a clause; under-crediting
    costs the publication.
```

## The one-line test
*If the source creator read this paragraph, would they feel quoted, credited, or robbed?* Publish only the first two.
